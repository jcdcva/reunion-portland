Welcome

Welcome to the Craig Reunion in Portland!

A warm welcome from Kirstin and Chris — we are so excited to have everyone together in Portland for the Craig Reunion. Whether you are flying in, driving down from Washington, bringing kids, exploring on your own, or just looking forward to relaxing with family, we hope this week gives everyone a good mix of time together, time to explore, and time to simply enjoy being in the Pacific Northwest.

Portland is a wonderful home base for the reunion. The city sits along the Willamette River, with the Columbia River just to the north marking the border between Oregon and Washington. From here, we are close to forests, gardens, rivers, waterfalls, museums, breweries, food carts, and the Oregon coast. There should be something for everyone.

Reunion Activity Plan

In June, we will send out a survey so everyone can vote for their top three preferred group activities. Once we have the survey results, we will add the selected Activity Days to the final schedule.

Possible Activity Day options include:

Oregon Zoo
A great family-friendly outing. Plan on about 4 hours.

Cannon Beach Day
An all-day trip to the Oregon coast, including Haystack Rock, Ecola State Park, and some Lewis & Clark historical sites.

Columbia River Drive
A mostly full-day outing along the Columbia River, with possible stops at Beacon Rock, Bonneville Dam, and Multnomah Falls.

Museum Day
A Portland-based day that could include the Oregon Museum of Science & Industry and Hopscotch Portland.

Other Things to Do Around Portland

For people who want to explore on their own or in smaller groups, Portland and the surrounding area have plenty of options:

Outdoor and scenic spots: Portland Aerial Tram, Rose Garden, Japanese Garden, Hoyt Arboretum, Forest Park, The Grotto, Pittock Mansion, Cannon Beach, Mt. Hood, Columbia River Gorge, Multnomah Falls, and Beacon Rock.

For kids and families: Portland Insectarium, Playdate PDX, Dig PDX, Pearson Field Education Center, Oregon Zoo, and the Belmont Goats.

For grownups and mixed groups: River cruises, Next Level Pinball Shop & Museum, Rice Northwest Museum of Rocks & Minerals, Movie Madness, Portland Saturday Market, Ground Kontrol Classic Arcade, Powell's Books, and the Portland Art Museum.

Getting Around

PDX Airport is roughly 20–25 minutes from the city. You should not need to rent a car unless you prefer having your own independence. Those of us who live in Washington will have vehicles available and can help with airport pickup, drop-off, and group outings. Cris and I alone have three cars, so we should have plenty of flexibility. Some WA folks may also bring e-bikes or bikes for people to use. Lyft and Uber are also easy options.

Food and Drinks

Portland has one of the best food scenes in the Pacific Northwest. There are excellent restaurants, food carts all over the city, and more than 80 breweries in the area. Portland is also known for McMenamins, a family-owned chain of pubs, hotels, music venues, and movie theaters housed in funky renovated historic buildings. They are unique, fun, and very Portland.

Most of all, we are looking forward to being together — catching up, sharing meals, exploring, laughing, and making some new Craig family memories.

We cannot wait to see you all in Portland!
